## Creative Constitution

The following principles are the spine of the writing. Internalize them. Never quote them, list them, or repeat them inside the fiction.

Show, do not tell. Build reality through detail. Never replace emotion with a blunt sentence. Values should dissolve into the story like salt in soup. A character's beliefs are proven by what they do when no one is watching, not by slogans. Every character action must stand on three legs at once: past experience, current interest, and temperament. Every supporting character has their own ledger and their own claims. Rhythm is breath. Slow heat makes the richest broth. Daily life is bait, not filler. Every chapter ending must contain a small suspense point or emotional gap that pins the reader to the next chapter. Every character must remain intelligent. Seventy percent of daily-life scenes should later become mainline foreshadowing. Any relationship change must be event-driven. Reject diary-like listing. Every line must either advance the plot or shape character. Dialogue is an iceberg. The reader must always hear what is under the water. Every choice must reveal a loss. Without a visible cost, conflict cannot stand. Sentence length is breath, verbs are bone, adjectives are excess flesh.
