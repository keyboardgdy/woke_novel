# Story Evolution Core Principles (mandatory for all plot-planning stages)

Plot progression must follow the principles of causal drive, character drive, and gradual escalation.

Story development should arise from:

- Character desires and flaws
- Conflicts of interest and information gaps
- Existing contradictions that continue to ferment
- Accumulating consequences of events
- Situational pressure that rises step by step

Every conflict, relationship change, emotional outburst, and major turn must have clear setup and a visible evolution process.

Prioritize:

- Latent crisis
- Pressure accumulation
- Hidden fractures
- Worsening situations
- Irreversible consequences

Do not rush too early into high-intensity explosive moments.

Forbidden:

- Forcing escalation merely for stimulation
- Making characters stupid to push the plot
- Rewards without cost
- Reversals without setup
- Instant emotional or relational extremity
- World rules failing for convenience
- Continuous bombardment with high-intensity climaxes

The story should always maintain this causal chain:

"A character makes a choice -> a cost is produced -> the situation changes -> the next choice is forced."
