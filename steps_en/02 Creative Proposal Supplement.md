## Core Task

Based on the selected creative proposal, supplement four dimensions of creative direction judgments, providing anchors for subsequent worldbuilding, character, story axis, and prose style.

**This is not** a thesis on creative writing. **It is** making four decisions: what flavor this book reads like, who it's for, what emotional register it strikes, and what it fundamentally expresses.

---

## Supplement Content

### 1. Prose Direction

Do not use labels (such as "classically elegant" or "lean and sharp"). Use concrete scales:

- Sentence tendency: short-dominant / alternating long and short / long-sentence exposition
- Register choice: colloquial / literary vernacular / semi-classical / mixed (specify which scenes switch)
- Rhetorical density: approximately how many metaphors/synesthesia per thousand characters; imagery-driven or sensory-driven
- Dialogue style: crisp and clean / with filler and pauses / mixed classical and modern
- Narrative POV tendency: tight single-POV / limited omniscient / rotating multi-POV

### 2. Target Audience

- Core reader profile: age range, reading habits, what experience they seek in this genre
- Core reader expectations: thrill / emotional immersion / mystery solving / world exploration / relationship entanglement — rank by priority
- Reader patience threshold: how many chapters of setup before the first payoff is acceptable

### 3. Emotional Tone

- Overall color temperature: cold / warm / alternating cold and warm (which scenes are cold, which are warm)
- Emotional texture: do not write "heavy and oppressive," write texture descriptions like "a gnawing anxiety lodged in the bones"
- Permitted emotional extremes: how dark at the darkest, how light at the lightest
- Main emotional line's temperature curve: how cold at the start, where the warming inflection point roughly falls

### 4. Core Theme

- One sentence clarifying what specific dilemma this book explores (not empty words like "growth" or "love" — a concrete paradox)
- How the theme is embodied through the protagonist's choices rather than narrated by the author
- The theme's gray areas: what is the correct answer? If there is none, which side will readers ultimately lean toward?

---

## Output Constraints

- Stay within 600–1100 characters, with a hard cap of 1500 characters
- Do not repeat content already in the creative proposal
- Do not expand into plot or preset specific events
- Each dimension's judgment must be concrete enough for downstream steps to directly reference
